From the Author-
Living the Lessons is the compelling drama of a determined teacher on a lofty quest: to learn to value every single one of her students—beyond a galaxy of stars! In a practical sense, it chronicles my philosophy of education: the Whole Child approach. If you are earnestly seeking ways to meet the social, emotional, intellectual and moral needs of the children in your life, please join with me.

Living the Lessons stands inexorably on the premise espoused by Saint Augustine, who believed that, “The value of a single child surpasses that of a galaxy of stars!” Long ago I happened upon his words in a book and was shaken to my very soul. Hauntingly, it seemed that Augustine was calling out to me from centuries gone by, gently urging me to examine my heart.

Clutching the book in my hands, I wept penitent tears as the bitter truth clamored for disclosure: Not only did I not value my students as Augustine exhorted, but I had favorites! The full truth be told, I had never even remotely considered the notion that I should value my students to such a degree.

On that pivotal day I solemnly vowed to change my course, and my life since has never been the same. Every moment of every day in the classroom I hold myself strictly accountable to that pledge. Understandably—especially considering today’s troubled times—choosing to follow Augustine’s calling has not been the easy path.

But despite the many times I have missed the mark with my students, each year, without exception, the path to loving children unconditionally grows less and less wearisome. Because I am learning to wholly value my students, I am, after thirty-seven years in the classroom, more enthusiastic than ever before.
